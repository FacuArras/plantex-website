Some common questions were often asked

How often should I water my plants?

It depends on the type of plant, but generally once a week is a good rule of thumb. Make sure to check the soil moisture level before watering to avoid overwatering.

What is the best type of soil for indoor plants?

Indoor plants thrive in well-draining soil that's rich in nutrients. Look for a potting mix that contains perlite, vermiculite, and peat moss.

How much sunlight do plants need?

Again, it depends on the type of plant. Some plants require direct sunlight for several hours a day, while others do better in indirect or low-light conditions. Make sure to research your specific plant's needs.

How do I know if my plant is getting enough nutrients?

Look for signs of growth and overall health. If your plant is growing well and has healthy leaves, it's likely getting enough nutrients. You can also add a slow-release fertilizer to the soil to ensure your plant is getting the nutrients it needs.

Can plants improve indoor air quality?

Yes, many plants have air-purifying properties that can help remove toxins from the air. Some of the best plants for air purification include snake plants, spider plants, and peace lilies.
